# Airline Customer Satisfaction Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes airline customer satisfaction using public datasets from the U.S. Department of Transportation (DoT) and Skytrax airline reviews.

The goal is to identify key factors that influence passenger satisfaction, understand traveler behaviors across different customer segments, and evaluate airline operational performance through complaint and delay data.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze airline customer satisfaction across major U.S. airlines
- Identify traveler demographics and travel patterns
- Evaluate satisfaction factors such as:
  - Seat comfort
  - Cabin staff service
  - In-flight entertainment
  - Wi-Fi connectivity
  - Food & beverages
  - Value for money
- Compare customer experiences across seat classes
- Examine flight delays and customer complaint trends
- Provide data-driven recommendations for improving customer experience

---

## Dataset

This project combines data from:

- U.S. Department of Transportation (DoT)
  - Air Travel Consumer Reports
  - Flight delays
  - Consumer complaints
  - On-time performance statistics

- Skytrax Airline Reviews
  - Passenger ratings
  - Satisfaction factor scores
  - Seat class information
  - Traveler type information

---

## Key Findings

- Leisure travelers account for the majority of airline passengers.
- Cabin staff service receives the highest satisfaction ratings.
- Wi-Fi, entertainment, and seat comfort are common passenger pain points.
- Business and First Class generally achieve higher satisfaction scores.
- Hawaiian Airlines demonstrates strong on-time performance and relatively low complaint volumes.
- Flight problems, baggage issues, and customer service are the leading causes of customer complaints.
- Customer reviews may exhibit self-selection bias and should be interpreted accordingly.
